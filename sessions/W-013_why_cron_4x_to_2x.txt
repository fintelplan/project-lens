W-013 — WHY: Cron cadence reduced 4x/day to 2x/day
====================================================

DATE:    2026-04-17
SESSION: LENS-013
COMMIT:  6e711bf (tag: v2x-daily-rollout)

WHAT CHANGED
------------
Four YAML workflows reduced from 4 cron lines to 2:
  .github/workflows/lens-collect.yml
  .github/workflows/lens-gdelt.yml
  .github/workflows/lens-manage-analyze.yml
  .github/workflows/lens-ref-free.yml

Kept: 13:28 UTC (DC 9:28 AM) + 01:28 UTC (DC 9:28 PM)
Dropped: 04:28 UTC + 17:28 UTC + 21:28 UTC (old 4x schedule)

Companion changes in same commit:
  code/lens_manager.py DAILY_BUDGET 4 -> 2
  code/lens_s2b_coordination.py timedelta(hours=6) -> timedelta(hours=12)

WHY WE DID IT
-------------
1. FREE TIER QUOTA MATH WAS FAILING UNDER 4x/DAY
   Each cron consumes ~30K Groq llama-3.3-70b tokens (MA + S2-A + S2-E + S2-GAP + S3-A).
   4 crons x 30K = 120K tokens/day.
   Groq free tier cap: 100K TPD (verified via official docs).
   Result: every day, crons #3 and #4 hit 429 partway through. Partial runs became the norm.

2. PHILOSOPHY: COMPLETE CYCLES > PARTIAL CYCLES
   DOC-006 Part 11: $0 free tier is a design constraint, not a limitation.
   Given the constraint, the choice is between:
     4 partial cycles per day (inconsistent, retry storms, MA save failures)
     2 complete cycles per day (clean MA, clean S4-E, clean S3-A)
   The canary needs to be HEARD, not just breathe more often.
   2 complete pulses beat 4 partial pulses for intelligence quality.

3. SYMMETRY GIVES BOTH SONNET REPORTS FRESH DATA
   Previous schedule: Sonnet 2 at 03:28 UTC reading MA from 21:28 UTC (6h old).
   New schedule:      Sonnet 2 at 03:28 UTC reading MA from 01:28 UTC (2h old).
   Both Sonnet reports now get equally fresh synthesis input.

4. BOTH CRONS DURING OPERATOR WAKING HOURS
   Bro Alpha in Thailand (UTC+7). Under old 4x, cron at 04:28 UTC = 11:28 AM Thai (watchable)
   but cron at 21:28 UTC = 04:28 AM Thai (asleep). Under new 2x, both crons are at
   20:28 Thai (evening) and 08:28 Thai (morning). Both watchable in real time.

WHY WE DID IT THIS WAY (NOT SOMETHING ELSE)
-------------------------------------------
ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED AND REJECTED:

  Option: Upgrade Groq Developer tier (~$3-5/month)
  Rejected: Breaks $0 free-tier philosophy. Paid capacity for work the free tier
            can actually do at 2x/day. LR-068 (evidence before upgrade) violated.

  Option: Create multiple Groq accounts (original T-05 plan)
  Rejected: Explicit Groq ToS violation. Multi-account to bypass quota = suspension risk.
            Verified via web search — Groq says "Groq's terms of service prohibit
            creating multiple accounts to circumvent rate limits."
            Recorded as LR-072.

  Option: Redistribute positions off llama-3.3-70b
  Rejected for now: 2-3 hours of careful work, risk of quality degradation on
                    S2-A/S2-E/S3-A if target model is weaker. May revisit in LENS-015+
                    IF 2x/day alone doesn't solve it.

  Option: Keep 4x/day, just accept partial runs
  Rejected: Observed behavior already shows MA saved 0 rows in 72 hours. System
            was producing less, not more, under 4x/day.

COUNTER-CHECK (WHAT COULD STILL GO WRONG)
-----------------------------------------
1. S3-A 7-day window now has 14 data points instead of 28 at 2x/day.
   Statistical power for pattern detection halved.
   Mitigation: accepted as tradeoff; monitor quality for 2 weeks per LR-068.
   If quality suffers, consider widening S3-A window to 14 days in LENS-015.

2. Pattern 2 (manufactured consensus) operates on 3-6 hour windows.
   Could a coordination burst happen entirely in the 12h gap and be missed?
   Mitigation: S2-B lookback widened 6h -> 12h (same patch). Each cron sees
   the full previous interval. No gaps.

3. Cycle label mapping function may return unexpected labels at 01 UTC.
   Mitigation: verified — lens_orchestrator.py _cycle() returns "night" for
   01 UTC (not a crashable value). analyze_lens.py and analyze_lens_multi.py
   have inconsistent mappings (pre-existing drift documented in LENS-013).
   Queued for LENS-014 unification (P5).

RULE BORN FROM THIS
-------------------
LR-073(A) — Lookback-Frequency Coupling
  "When cron frequency changes, audit every time-window lookback in downstream
   reading code for density preservation. A lookback of N hours paired with
   a frequency of every M hours requires N >= M to avoid silent coverage gaps.
   Widen lookbacks BEFORE deploying the frequency change, in the same patch."
